The Process

4 Steps from Face to Identity

FaceID SaaS uses a deep learning pipeline that converts human faces into unique mathematical fingerprints for instant identification.

Step 01
Face Detection
The SSD MobileNetV1 model scans every video frame in real-time, drawing bounding boxes around every human face it finds — no matter the lighting or angle.
Step 02
Landmark Detection
68 precise facial landmark points are mapped onto each detected face — eyes, nose, mouth, jaw, eyebrows — creating a structural skeleton of your face.
Step 03
Feature Extraction
A deep neural network converts the face into a 128-dimensional vector — a mathematical fingerprint unique to every individual, stored in your database.
Step 04
Identity Matching
Euclidean distance between the live face vector and stored vectors determines if it's a match. Below 0.55 threshold = recognized person with confidence score.

Step 2 — AI Landmark Mapping
Step 2 of 4

68-Point Facial Mapping

Once a face is detected, face-api.js's landmark model places 68 precise points across your face — covering the eye contours, eyebrow arches, nose bridge, nostril positions, lip edges, and jawline.

These landmarks are used to align the face, compensating for rotation and slight tilt, before passing it to the recognition neural network.

Step 3 — Identity Confirmed
Step 3 of 4

Real-time Identity Matching

The recognized face descriptor (128 numbers) is compared against all stored descriptors using Euclidean distance. The closest match below 0.55 distance is declared the recognized person.

Confidence % = (1 - distance) × 100. A score above 45% with distance below threshold means the person is identified. Results update every 200ms for seamless live recognition.

128-dim vectors Euclidean distance 0.55 threshold 200ms refresh

Step 4 — ESP32 Response
Step 4 of 4

Hardware ESP32 Integration

Recognition results are instantly sent to your ESP32 microcontroller via HTTP. Known faces trigger the green LED. Unknown faces trigger the red LED plus a buzzer alert for security awareness.

A smart debounce system prevents rapid-fire signals, ensuring the ESP32 receives clear, stable commands — even when multiple faces are in frame simultaneously.

HTTP real-time signal Green / Red LED Buzzer alert Smart debounce
